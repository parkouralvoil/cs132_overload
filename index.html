With the rising trend of electric vehicles and hybrid cars in the Philippines our project seeks to compare the cost efficiency of these vehicles compared to gas vehicles. We compare prices of fuel, prices of electricity, and the fuel efficiency of gas/electric/hybrid cars. We also consider the traffic present in NCR to determine the average fuel efficiency of these cars.

Research Questions

  1. What is the trend of the costs of kilowatt-hours per kilometer VS kilometer per gallon given the gas and electricity prices in the Philippines (NCR)?
  2. How will the greenhouse gas emissions (NCR) of gas vehicles change as more and more EVs replace gas vehicles overtime?

Hypothesis

  • Null: Automobile power source has no effect on cost efficiency
  • Alternative: Automobile power source has an effect on cost efficiency

Data

We gathered data from these sites:

  • MMDA - Annual Average Daily Traffic, 2012 to 2023
  • MERALCO Rates Archive
  • NCR Prevailing Retail Pump Prices | Department of Energy Philippines
  • Data Request: Vehicle and License Registrations (2019-2024)

We used the code of Team R.B.C from the 2023-2024 iteration of CS 132 to scrape the data from the MERALCO Rates Archive website. This includes data from 2010 to 2024.
We then made our own scraper to convert the PDF tables from the Pump Prices website into CSV files. This currently only includes data from every week in 2024 up until 2025, but we plan to include data from 2020 up to 2023 as well for this dataset.
Lastly, we manually compiled the data from MMDA - Annual Average Daily Traffic, and the Vehicle and License Registrations. This dataset only has data for years 2012 to 2023.

Data Preprocessing

We plan to drop rows that mostly contain NULL values. For rows with only a few null values, we will be replacing them with the average value in that column to maintain the info stored in the other non-empty columns of that row. Moreover, we drop irrelevant columns to ensure that the data only has relevant columns. Examples of relevant columns include: total bill per kWh, common price of pump gas, and volume for non-trucks/motorcycles.
The Pump Prices data from 2020 to 2023 does not contain the summary information unlike the more recent datasets. Hence we plan to first convert the entire table into a CSV. This will give us the raw data of gas prices from every city in the NCR. Afterwards, we will then summarize the datas in these CSV files to keep consistent with the summarized data from 2024 and 2025.
